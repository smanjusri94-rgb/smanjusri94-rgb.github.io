Project Overview

This project focuses on the design and analysis of a telescopic cascode differential amplifier with full transistor-level biasing circuits. The design meets stringent specifications on gain, bandwidth, PSRR, CMRR, and slew rate under realistic load conditions using Cadence Spectre simulations.

Circuit Architecture

Complete schematic of the telescopic cascode differential amplifier including bias generation circuits (Vbp1, Vbp2, Vbn1, Vbn2). The topology is optimized for high gain and low power consumption while maintaining sufficient output swing.

AC Performance – Gain & Bandwidth

Gain versus frequency response for different input common-mode voltages (1.5V–2V). The amplifier achieves a DC gain exceeding 50 dB and a unity-gain bandwidth greater than 50 MHz with a 1 pF load capacitor.

Stability Analysis

Phase margin plot at VCM = 1.5 V and VDD = 3 V. Adequate phase margin confirms stable closed-loop operation under nominal bias conditions.

PSRR & CMRR Performance

PSRR+ response showing strong rejection of supply noise from the positive supply rail across frequency.

PSRR− response demonstrating robustness against noise injected from the negative supply rail.

Common-mode rejection ratio (CMRR) plot indicating excellent suppression of common-mode signals across the operating bandwidth.

Transient & Slew Rate Analysis

Slew rate measurement in unity-gain configuration using a large-signal step input. The measured slew rate is approximately 1.93 MV/s, ensuring accurate tracking of fast-changing input signals without distortion.
