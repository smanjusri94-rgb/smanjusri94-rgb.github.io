Single-Ended NMOS Folded Cascode Amplifier with Class-AB Output Buffer

Advanced Analog IC Design (EEE523) • Cadence Virtuoso Implementation

Project Overview

This project involves the design and comprehensive evaluation of a single-ended NMOS input folded cascode amplifier integrated with a Class-AB output buffer. The amplifier targets low-power operation while achieving high gain, wide bandwidth, and strong linearity.

The design was implemented at the transistor level and verified using extensive simulations in Cadence Virtuoso, covering transient, AC, distortion, noise, and stability analyses.

Fig. 1 – Single-ended NMOS folded cascode amplifier with Class-AB output buffer

Design Specifications

  • Supply Voltage (VDD): 3 V
  • Quiescent Power Dissipation: ~2 mW
  • Total Bias Current: ~666 µA
  • Input Common-Mode Range: 1.5 V – 2.5 V
  • Load Conditions: Resistive and capacitive (1 pF – 200 pF)

Tools & Software Used

  • Cadence Virtuoso: Schematic capture and hierarchical circuit design
  • Cadence ADE (L/XL): Transient, AC, noise, and distortion simulations
  • Spectre Simulator: Accurate transistor-level circuit analysis
  • Analog Design Techniques: Hand analysis, small-signal modeling, and bias optimization

Simulation Results & Analysis

1. Transient Response & Output Swing

The amplifier was configured as an inverting amplifier with 0 dB gain. Transient simulations verified the output swing and large-signal behavior.

  • Achieved 1 Vpp output swing
  • Stable operation across VCM sweep

Fig. 2 – Transient output waveform demonstrating 1 Vpp swing

2. Harmonic Distortion Analysis

Distortion performance was evaluated using a 5 kHz sinusoidal input under mid-supply common-mode conditions.

  • HD3 ≈ 50.6 dB
  • Measured using FFT spectrum analysis in ViVA

Fig. 3 – Frequency spectrum showing third-order harmonic distortion

3. AC Gain, Bandwidth & Stability

  • Open-loop DC gain: ~98 dB
  • Unity Gain Frequency: > 10 MHz
  • Phase Margin: ~61°
  • Gain Margin: ~26 dB

Fig. 4 – AC magnitude and phase response of the amplifier

4. Noise, PSRR & CMRR

  • Input-referred noise floor: ≤ 15 nV/√Hz
  • PSRR @ 10 kHz: > 70 dB
  • CMRR @ 10 kHz: > 70 dB

Layout & Matching Considerations

Special emphasis was placed on transistor matching and symmetry to minimize offset, noise, and distortion.

  • Careful matching of differential input devices
  • Interdigitated current mirrors in bias circuits
  • Symmetric NMOS and PMOS current paths
  • Minimized signal coupling through strategic device placement

Fig. 5 – Layout and device matching strategy (conceptual)

Conclusion

This design demonstrates a low-power, high-gain analog amplifier meeting stringent performance requirements for gain, stability, noise, and distortion. The folded cascode architecture combined with a Class-AB buffer provides an effective balance between performance and power efficiency, making it suitable for precision analog systems.
